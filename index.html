About Us

Logotech Clothing, a privately-owned company based in Kolkata, West Bengal, India, is dedicated to the sustainable procurement and production of textile goods. At Logotech Clothing, we prioritize building strong partnerships with our employees, customers, vendors, and all those associated with us or interested in joining our community.

While our journey began as a T-shirt manufacturing unit in 2002, our expertise now lies in producing Kids Wear, and we have also excelled in Women's Resort wear through our in-house brand, CHANGE! established in 2008. Through collaborations with esteemed global brands, we have garnered extensive knowledge and exposure, leading to significant growth over the past 21 years. We are proud to have worked with designer labels, brands, retail companies, store chains, from across the globe with our clients based in Europe, U.K, U.S.

We take pride in our certifications, including GOTS, GRS, and Fairtrade, which reflect our commitment to sustainable practices.

The company offers services of end-to-end manufacturing, from fabric sourcing, printing, cutting and sewing. The products cater to numerous quality check certifications before their dispatch, producing over 25,000 pcs per month. Being a mass production company, Logotech recognises the role it plays in the ecology of the Fashion Industry.

The factory follows internationally benchmarked guidelines for pollution control and energy conservation. Committed to socially responsible work practices, it is ensured that no level of child labour is involved in any stage of production and a clean production environment to maintain health levels for all workers. Logotech adheres to SA 8000 and a Fairtrade compliance within 2008.

Furthermore, Logotech actively strives to do more to employ women and offer them stable and long-term job opportunities. The company believes its karigars to be the spine of the organisation and plays an active role in providing an accommodating and welcoming workplace to them, including holding health and yoga camps for the workers throughout the pandemic and celebration of local festivals in the organisation to maintain morale even at ground level.
